About the Stone

What is Jaisalmer Sandstone?

Jaisalmer sandstone is a premium natural stone quarried from the Thar Desert region of Jaisalmer, Rajasthan, India. Its distinctive warm golden-yellow color — caused by naturally occurring iron oxide — has made it the stone of choice for Indian temple builders, palace architects, and contemporary designers for over a thousand years.

The stone is also known as Jaisalmer yellow stone, golden sandstone, or desert gold. The famous Jaisalmer Fort (Sonar Quila) — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — was built entirely from this stone and has retained its warm golden glow for over 800 years, a testament to its exceptional durability.

FAQ

Common Questions

How is Jaisalmer sandstone different from marble or granite?
Jaisalmer sandstone has a warmer, more textured aesthetic than marble or granite. It is softer and easier to carve — making it ideal for intricate jali and sculptural work. It provides better thermal insulation. It is more affordable for large-area coverage and has deep cultural significance in Indian architecture. Unlike marble and granite, it cannot be highly polished but achieves a beautiful honed matte finish.
