The Sacred Stone

Why Jaisalmer Stone
for Temples?

Jaisalmer sandstone has been used in the construction and decoration of Hindu temples for over a thousand years. Its warm golden-yellow color represents purity, prosperity, and divine radiance in Hindu tradition. The stone's workability — it is soft enough to carve intricately but durable enough to last centuries — makes it the ideal material for temple architecture.

Rajasthan's most iconic temples, including those in Jaisalmer, Jodhpur, Ranakpur, and Mount Abu, are built from this very stone. At Shraddha Saburi Stone, we supply premium temple-grade sandstone for new temple construction, renovation, and heritage restoration projects across India and internationally.

Our master shilpis (stone artisans) work alongside modern CNC machinery to produce pillar capitals, jali screens, shikhars, mandap elements, torana gates, step platforms, and deity figures to exact specifications. We work with temple trusts, religious organizations, and architects to bring your temple vision to life.

FAQ

Common Questions

What stone is traditionally used in Indian temples?
Jaisalmer sandstone (and other Rajasthani sandstones) have been the primary stone for North Indian temple construction for over a thousand years. The stone's warm golden color, workability for intricate carving, and proven durability across centuries make it the most revered temple building stone in India.
Can you supply stone for a new temple construction project?
Yes, we supply complete temple stone packages including structural elements, decorative carvings, jali screens, pillars, and flooring. We work directly with temple trusts, managing committees, and construction contractors. Send us your architectural drawings for a detailed quotation.
Do you have experience with both traditional and modern temple designs?
Yes. We are experienced with traditional Nagara, Vesara, and Dravida temple styles as well as contemporary Hindu temple designs. Our shilpis are trained in traditional iconography and can produce both classical and modern interpretations in Jaisalmer sandstone.
