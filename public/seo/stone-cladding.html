About Stone Cladding

Jaisalmer Sandstone Wall Cladding

Stone cladding refers to thin panels of natural stone (typically 12mm to 50mm thick) used to cover walls both inside and outside buildings. Jaisalmer sandstone cladding is among the most popular choices for its warm golden color, natural texture, excellent durability, and easy maintenance.

Unlike full-thickness stone construction, cladding provides the beauty and prestige of natural stone at lower weight and cost. It can be applied to virtually any structural wall using cement mortar, stone adhesive, or mechanical fixing systems. It requires no load-bearing capacity from the wall it covers.

Our cladding is precision-cut at our Jaisalmer factory in standard and custom sizes with a range of surface finishes. We supply cladding for residential villas, luxury hotels, commercial buildings, heritage restoration, and landscape projects across India, UAE, USA, and UK.

Cladding FAQ

Common Questions

What thickness of stone cladding is recommended for exterior walls?
For exterior walls, we recommend 25–50mm thickness for durability and weather resistance. Thinner panels (12–20mm) are suitable for sheltered exterior walls or interior applications. Exterior cladding should be installed with proper waterproofing, expansion joints, and appropriate fixing methods.
How is stone cladding installed?
Stone cladding can be installed using cement mortar (traditional method), stone adhesive (for lighter panels), or mechanical fixing systems (stainless steel brackets for heavy panels). The wall surface should be clean, level, and structurally sound. Interior cladding is typically done with adhesive while exterior cladding often uses both adhesive and mechanical fixings.
Do you supply cladding for high-rise buildings?
Yes, we supply stone cladding for multi-storey residential and commercial buildings. For high-rise applications, we recommend thinner, lighter panels (12–20mm) with mechanical fixing systems designed for wind load and thermal movement. We can provide material specifications required by structural engineers for such projects.
