UAE Market

Why UAE Buyers Choose Jaisalmer Sandstone

The UAE construction sector has grown at a remarkable pace, with ongoing demand for premium building materials that marry luxury aesthetics with practicality. Jaisalmer yellow sandstone has become a preferred choice among UAE architects, interior designers, and real estate developers for several distinct reasons.

Luxury Construction Demand — Dubai and Abu Dhabi's ongoing residential villa, hotel, and commercial development boom creates consistent demand for distinctive, high-quality natural stone facades, flooring, and decorative elements.
Indian Stone Heritage in the Gulf — Jaisalmer sandstone's warm golden colour and intricate carving tradition resonate well with the region's architectural sensibility — a natural complement to the sandy desert palette of the UAE landscape.
Cost vs Italian Marble — Premium imported Italian marble can cost USD 20–40/sqft or more. Jaisalmer sandstone delivers a comparable luxury appearance at USD 3.50–8.00/sqft FOB, offering significant savings on large villa and hospitality projects.
Proximity & Short Transit — India's western coast ports (Mundra, JNPT) provide one of the shortest shipping routes to Jebel Ali, Dubai. The 8–14 day sea transit is far shorter than stone sourced from Europe or South America.
Versatility of Forms — Jaisalmer sandstone is available not just as tiles and slabs but as hand-carved jali screens, decorative pillars, wall panels, and bespoke architectural elements — meeting the full range of luxury project requirements from a single source.

Frequently Asked

UAE Export Questions Answered

How do I import Jaisalmer sandstone from India to the UAE?

The process is straightforward. Contact us via WhatsApp or email with your product specifications and quantity. We will provide a proforma invoice with FOB Mundra pricing. Once you confirm the order and pay the advance, we begin production and packaging. We arrange the shipping line booking and provide all export documents — commercial invoice, packing list, certificate of origin, and HS code declaration — for smooth UAE customs clearance at Jebel Ali Port.

What is the customs duty on sandstone imported into the UAE?

The UAE imposes a standard 5% customs duty on most imported building materials, including natural stone. This applies to the CIF (cost, insurance, freight) value of the shipment. Free zone imports may have different rules. We recommend consulting your UAE-based clearing agent for the exact applicable duty based on the current HS code classification for sandstone (typically HS 6802 for worked stone).

How long does shipping from India to Dubai take?

Transit time from Mundra Port (Gujarat, India) to Jebel Ali Port (Dubai) is approximately 8–14 days by sea freight, depending on the shipping line and vessel schedule. Adding production lead time of 10–21 working days from order confirmation, buyers should plan for a total door-to-port timeline of 3–5 weeks from order placement. Air freight is available for small samples or urgent orders at significantly higher cost.

Which port does Jaisalmer sandstone arrive at in Dubai?

Sandstone shipments from Jaisalmer are routed via Mundra Port or JNPT (Nhava Sheva, Mumbai) and arrive at Jebel Ali Port in Dubai — the UAE's primary commercial port and one of the largest container ports in the world. From Jebel Ali, goods can be transported by road to any UAE emirate including Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, Ajman, Ras Al Khaimah, and Fujairah.
