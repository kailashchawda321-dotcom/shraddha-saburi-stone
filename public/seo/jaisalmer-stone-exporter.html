Export Overview

Jaisalmer Sandstone Export to the World

Shraddha Saburi Stone is a registered exporter of Jaisalmer yellow sandstone with an active IEC (Import Export Code) and established freight relationships with CHA (Customs House Agent) partners at Mundra Port in Gujarat — India's primary container port for Rajasthan stone exports. Our export operations have supplied sandstone to architects, landscape designers, temple trusts, and stone importers across the Gulf, North America, Europe, Australia, and East Africa.

Jaisalmer sandstone is exported as cut tiles and slabs for cladding and flooring, as rough blocks for in-country processing, as finished architectural elements (jali screens, pillars, balustrades, corbels), and as complete temple kits. Each category has different HS code classifications, packaging requirements, and quality grading standards — our export team manages all of these without burdening the customer.

Our standard export grade stone is quarried from the consistent yellow-gold belt of Jaisalmer district, inspected at our facility for colour uniformity, absence of iron bleed stains, and structural soundness. A random sample from every consignment is retained for three months after shipment to facilitate any post-delivery quality claims. We provide a quality test report from an accredited Indian laboratory with every export consignment, covering compressive strength, water absorption, modulus of rupture, and abrasion resistance.

For new export customers, we strongly recommend ordering a sample set (5–10 pieces in different finishes and thicknesses) before placing a full commercial order. Sample sets are shipped by air courier within 3–5 days of payment and give you a reliable representation of the actual export material colour and quality.

Export Process

How Export Orders Are Processed

Our export process is streamlined for international buyers who may never visit our facility. It begins with a WhatsApp or email inquiry in which the buyer shares their stone type, finish, dimensions, quantity, and destination port. We respond within 24 hours with a pro-forma invoice including FOB Mundra pricing, estimated freight cost (indicative, confirmed by our freight forwarder), and lead time.

Upon receipt of the advance payment (typically 30–50% by T/T bank transfer), we commence production. Large quarry block orders proceed immediately; finished cut or carved orders go into our production schedule. We send mid-production photographs via WhatsApp for the buyer to approve colour and quality before final packing. Final balance payment is made against a scan of the Bill of Lading, which we release via our freight forwarder typically within 48 hours of the vessel's departure from Mundra.

We maintain relationships with three international freight forwarders who compete on rates for each consignment, ensuring our buyers receive competitive ocean freight pricing. For buyers with their own preferred freight forwarder, we seamlessly hand over to their agent at the Mundra port gate on a strict FOB basis.

Frequently Asked
Which countries do you export Jaisalmer sandstone to?
We export Jaisalmer sandstone to over 15 countries including UAE, USA, UK, Germany, France, Australia, Canada, Singapore, New Zealand, South Africa, Kenya, Tanzania, Bahrain, Qatar, and Oman. Our largest export markets are the UAE for landscape and hospitality construction, the USA for Hindu temple projects, and the UK for heritage restoration. We can export to any country that permits natural stone imports — contact us with your destination and we will confirm logistics and documentation requirements.
What export documentation do you provide?
We provide a complete set of export documents with every shipment: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading (or Airway Bill for smaller consignments), Certificate of Origin issued by the Rajasthan Chamber of Commerce and Industry, Phytosanitary Certificate where required, and a Stone Quality Test Report from an accredited Indian laboratory. For GST refund documentation, we provide fully compliant GST invoices. For temple projects in the USA and UK, we provide HS Code classification letters and material data sheets required by customs.
How is Jaisalmer sandstone packaged for international shipping?
Cut tiles and slabs are stacked in wooden crates with foam interleaving between each piece to prevent surface scratching during ocean transit. Carved items — jali screens, pillars, corbels, and architectural elements — are individually wrapped in bubble film, placed in bespoke timber crates with internal bracing, and packed with wood shavings or foam pellets for shock absorption. All crates are fumigated and ISPM-15 marked for international phytosanitary compliance. Standard 20ft and 40ft FCL containers are used for large orders; LCL consolidation is available for smaller sample and trial orders.
