The Art of Jali

What is Stone Jali?

Stone jali (also spelled 'jaali' or 'jali') is the ancient Indian art of creating intricately perforated stone screens. Jaisalmer's stone craftsmen have perfected this art over centuries, creating lattice screens that filter light beautifully while allowing air circulation — a natural solution perfectly adapted to the hot desert climate.

Jali screens are characterized by geometric patterns (hexagonal, octagonal, circular, star-shaped), floral and vine motifs, Islamic-inspired arabesque patterns, and custom designs. The patterns vary from simple repeating grids to extraordinarily complex compositions with multiple layers of meaning.

At Shraddha Saburi Stone, we produce jali screens using both traditional hand-carving by master shilpis and modern CNC machinery. CNC ensures perfect geometric precision for contemporary designs, while hand-carving produces the character and subtlety of traditional craftsmanship.

Jali FAQ

Common Questions

What jali designs are available?
We offer traditional Rajasthani geometric patterns (chakra, hexagon, octagon, star), floral and vine designs, Islamic-inspired arabesque screens, modern minimalist grid patterns, and fully custom designs from your artwork or CAD files. Both standard sizes and custom panels are available.
What is the standard thickness for stone jali?
Standard stone jali panels are 38mm to 50mm thick. Thinner panels (25mm) are available for interior use with lighter patterns. Thicker panels (60–75mm) are recommended for exterior facades and structural screens. The thickness is determined by the pattern complexity and panel size.
Can you make jali to match a specific pattern or heritage design?
Yes. We regularly reproduce heritage jali patterns from photographs or drawings. We can match traditional designs from Rajasthani havelis, Mughal architecture, and temple screens. Provide us with detailed photographs and measurements and our team will produce matching panels.
